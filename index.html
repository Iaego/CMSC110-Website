A Taste of Our Heritage

FOOD

Etag

Cured, aged, and smoked. Etag is frequently used in stews, soups, fried rice, and even as a savory stuffing.

Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan (chicken) has a subtle burnt or smoky taste, and can have a tough or chewy texture. Accompanied by a savory and aromatic, and slightly sour soup.

Binungor

Rich, hot and spicy stew made with agurong (water snails), rabong (bamboo shoots), and chili peppers.

DRINKS

Tapey

A sweet, strong, and full-bodied alcoholic drink made from fermented glutinous rice.

Safeng

A sour fermented tonic made from camote, corn, cassava, and chicken or pork bones.
